Rules & Regulations

In order to keep SFUF as safe as possible, we want to hold everyone to the same standards. We expect active participation in taking care of one another, in doing the right thing, in making smart decisions. So much hard work has gone into making this event beautiful and comfortable, and it will take each of you to keep it up throughout the event. Thank you so much for your attendance, and...

  • Please respect the land at all times.
  • Recycling centers will be provided throughout the property, and it is your responsibility to ensure that everything makes it to the designated area. We are asking you to be conscious with disposal with our new recycling / composting efforts. There are tin cans scattered as well for cigarette butts, blunt or joint roaches, and ashing bowls. Pack it in, pack it out.
  • Power sources are extremely limited and for the use of production for the festival.
  • This a natural area, please be very careful! Wear shoes, watch out for blackberry bushes and other painful plants, bugs, uneven ground, holes, tree roots.
  • There are porta potties on site, available for use. Please respect that space, keeping it as clean as possible, as others will need to use them the entirety of the weekend.

YES:

  • Tents, sleeping bags, pop up coverage, chairs, etc.
  • Water bottle - Metal bottles available for purchase. Please don’t bring plastic bottles.
  • 3 days worth of food / ice - Many vendors will be offering a wide variety of foods / goods
  • Beverages - Recycling will be offered for cans, no glass
  • Flashlights / headlamps - Many of the trails and pathways between stages will not be lit
  • Herbal bug spray / anti-itch topical
  • Clothing by the layer (be prepared for possible chilly nights and warm days!)
  • Bikes (must have lights for night rides)
  • Cash for vendors - NO ATM on site
  • Costumes! Opt out of plastic glitter.
  • Art supplies
  • Shareables! Presents, gifts, trades, etc.
  • LIGHT & LOVE!

NO:

  • Vehicle re-entry
  • NO illegal drugs, substances, or paraphernalia, including but not limited to whip-its
  • Weapons
  • Bad vibes
  • Glass bottles
  • Dogs/animals
  • Sound systems (small bluetooth speakers are okay)
  • NO UNDERAGE DRINKING/SMOKING (Security enforced)

Peace Keepers

Peace Keepers are an organized team of responsible eyes & ears to watch over our dancing souls. They will be rockin' green and orange tie dye, with fanny packs full of supplies! They have headquarters called The Zen Den, which is a safe zone for anybody needing rest. Look to them for any information needed, directional questions, and safety!
